Session One: Beginner Investor Mistakes & Problems

  • The investor thinks he or she needs special schooling, a degree, or to become a real estate agent in order to be an investor
  • The investor has no money or financial resources
  • The investor does not treat real estate investing as a business
  • The investor starts with the beginning in sight not the end in mind
  • The investor thinks the real estate agent will teach him or her the process of real estate investing
  • The investor tries to do everything on her own and doesn’t want to work with any professionals
  • The investor believes not knowing something or claiming ignorance will be an adequate defense
  • The investor thinks buying the cheapest houses in the worst areas is always a great investment
  • The investor doesn’t understand real estate investing is oftentimes about being creative
  • The investor wastes valuable time by not getting started today

Session Three: Investor Financing Mistakes & Problems

  • The investor doesn’t realize that financing is usually the number 1 roadblock to real estate investing
  • The investor has no money or financial resources
  • The investor doesn’t know if he or she is working with a lender or a loan broker
  • The investor is seeking out a rehab loan with a traditional lender
  • The investor tries to fit the scope of work into the deal itself
  • The investor doesn’t know about the MLS listing problem
  • The investor doesn’t understand the value of using other people’s money (OPM)
  • The investor’s exit strategy doesn’t make sense, or worse the investor doesn’t have any exit strategy
  • The investor is seeking 100% financing with no money into the deal
  • The investor plans to live in the subject property

Session Four: Contractor Mistakes & Problems

  • The investor pays a contractor upfront
  • The investor doesn’t know what should be included in the written contractor’s agreement
  • The investor doesn’t know about the final 5% -10% problem
  • The investor worries he or she will look like a novice
  • The investor doesn’t check local civil court records
  • The investor doesn’t have a specific payment or draw process
  • The investor doesn’t have a per diem fee for the contractor not completing the project on time
  • The investor doesn’t use change orders or get changes in writing
  • The investor doesn’t do a final walkthrough with the contractor before making the final payment
  • The investor gets nickeled and dimed

Session Five: Landlording Mistakes & Problems

  • The investor discriminates based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status or handicap
  • The investor doesn’t know what to do when a tenant damages the property
  • The investor doesn’t understand cash always flows but sometimes in the wrong direction
  • The investor is a hands off out of state investor
  • The investor doesn’t check local civil court records as part of the standard application processing
  • The investor allows a tenant to make repairs or work on the rental property in exchange for rent
  • The investor allows a non-paying tenant to remain in the property too long before taking any action
  • The investor doesn’t know that property management is oftentimes the make it or break it of owning a rental
  • The investor doesn’t know to take advantage of the many free or cheap marketing platforms for rental property

Session Six: Flipping Mistakes & Problems

  • The investor underestimates the scope of work or budget for the repairs
  • The investor doesn’t know the true numbers or how to really calculate expected profit
  • The investor doesn’t know to file important local documentation
  • The investor doesn’t know about the possible deed restriction
  • The scope of work isn’t appropriate for the neighborhood or marketplace
  • The investor doesn’t budget any money to start the work in the rehab project while waiting on the first draw
  • The investor doesn’t notice the neighbors
  • The investor thinks buying the cheapest houses in the worst areas is always a great investment
  • The investor doesn’t consider the tax implications of flipping
  • The investor thinks if flipping works at one price point it must work at a higher price point too
